What is Coronavirus?

Coronaviruses are a type of virus. There are many different kinds, and some cause disease. A newly identified coronavirus, SARS-CoV-2, has caused a worldwide pandemic of respiratory illness, called COVID-19.

COVID-19 emerged in China in December 2019 and has since spread globally, affecting millions of people worldwide.

How Does the New Coronavirus Spread?

COVID-19 spreads primarily through close contact with someone who is infected. When an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks, they release respiratory droplets that can be inhaled by others nearby.

Main Transmission Methods:

  • Person-to-person close contact (within 6 feet)
  • Respiratory droplets from coughing, sneezing, or talking
  • Touching surfaces with the virus and then touching your face
  • Possible airborne transmission in enclosed spaces

Wash Hands

For at least 20 seconds with soap and water

Wear Mask

Especially in public or crowded places

Physical Distance

Stay at least 6 feet from others

Sanitize

Use hand sanitizer when soap is unavailable
